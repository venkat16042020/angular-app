Pomegranate Disease Management Guide

Bacterial Blight (Xanthomonas axonopodis)

Symptoms: Water-soaked, black or brown angular spots on leaves and dark, sunken lesions on fruit, often leading to fruit dropping.

Solution: Prune and destroy infected branches. Apply Streptomycin sulfate with Copper oxychloride as per local agricultural guidelines. Avoid water-logging.

Anthracnose & Fungal Spots

Symptoms: Irregular black or dark brown spots on leaves and calyx, exacerbated by high humidity and rain.

Solution: Space trees adequately for air circulation. Spray organic fungicides like Copper oxychloride or Mancozeb during the pre-fruiting stage.

Pests (Aphids, Mealybugs, Fruit Borer)

Symptoms: Aphids cause honeydew and leaf curling, while fruit borers eat the flesh inside.

Solution: Spray Neem oil for light infestations. For fruit borers, remove affected fruit and use recommended bio-control agents or safe chemical insecticides.

Fruit Splitting/Cracking

Symptoms: Fruit cracks open, often caused by irregular watering or sudden weather shifts.

Solution: Ensure a consistent irrigation schedule to maintain even soil moisture.

Nutrient Deficiencies

Symptoms: Inter-veinal chlorosis (yellowing between veins) on younger leaves typically points to Iron deficiency, while curling or drying tips can indicate Boron or Zinc issues.
